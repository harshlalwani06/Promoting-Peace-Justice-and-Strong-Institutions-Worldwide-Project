Sustainable Development

Sustainable development aims to meet present needs without compromising future generations. In 2015, world leaders adopted the 2030 Agenda with 17 SDGs to end poverty, protect the planet, and reduce inequalities. These goals provide a practical path to address violent conflict, human rights abuses, climate change, and environmental degradation, ensuring no one is left behind and fostering a better future for all.

Goal 16 Peace, Justice & Strong Institutions

Peace, Justice, and Strong Institutions (Goal 16) aim to promote peaceful, inclusive societies, provide access to justice for all, and build effective, accountable institutions. This goal focuses on reducing violence, ensuring responsive governance, combating corruption, and protecting fundamental freedoms. Achieving Goal 16 fosters stability and development, ensuring a just and equitable society where every individual’s rights are respected and upheld.

Aims

Goal 16 aims to reduce violence, strengthen institutions, and promote human rights. It fosters peace through education and art, ends abuse and exploitation, and amplifies marginalized voices. The goal strengthens global partnerships against terrorism and organized crime while safeguarding fundamental freedoms, ensuring all individuals can live without fear of persecution or discrimination.

Facts-Figures

Geopolitical tensions and nationalism challenge global cooperation, stressing the need for joint efforts to aid developing nations. In 2020, data funding dropped significantly. Despite record ODA in 2022, much aid went to refugees and Ukraine. Internet access improved by 65% since 2015, with 66% online in 2022, yet the digital divide persists, especially for women. Sustained efforts are vital for equitable access to data and technology.

Target & Indicators

Goal 16 targets reducing violence, protecting children, ensuring equal justice, combating organized crime, and reducing corruption. It promotes transparency, effective institutions, inclusive decision-making, and global governance. The goal ensures public access to information, protects fundamental freedoms, strengthens institutions to prevent violence and crime, and enforces non-discriminatory laws for peace, justice, and strong institutions.
